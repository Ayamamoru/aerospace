Thermal Extraction with Solar Energy

The most promising approach uses concentrated solar energy to heat regolith and release water vapor. This method has been successfully tested by NASA and requires minimal equipment.

How It Works

  1. Collection: Robotic excavators gather ice-rich regolith from permanently shadowed craters
  2. Heating: Solar concentrators or electric heaters raise temperature to 100-200¬∞C
  3. Vaporization: Water sublimates from the regolith and enters collection chamber
  4. Condensation: Vapor is cooled and condensed into liquid water in shaded collectors
  5. Purification: Electrolysis separates H‚ÇÇO into hydrogen and oxygen for fuel/life support

Dry Drilling Technology

Advanced drilling systems can extract ice directly from subsurface deposits without surface excavation. China's Chang'e missions have pioneered this approach for lunar resource extraction.
