The Challenge: Sustainable Space Exploration

Deep space exploration will benefit humanity through technological innovations, advancements in scientific knowledge, economic growth, and inspiration for future generations. However, launching all necessary resources from Earth is prohibitively expensive and unsustainable for long-term missions.

The discovery of water ice on the Moon changes everything. With billions of tons of water ice near the lunar south pole, we have the opportunity to establish a self-sustaining lunar economy that can support permanent human presence and serve as a launching point for Mars missions.

Our Project Vision

We propose developing a comprehensive lunar water mining and processing system that will:

  • Extract water ice from permanently shadowed craters (PSCs) on the lunar south pole
  • Process water into drinkable water, breathable oxygen, and rocket fuel
  • Support NASA's Artemis missions and future lunar habitats
  • Make humans a multiplanetary species by reducing dependency on Earth's resources

Why the Moon First?

The Moon is the first stop in humanity's deep-space exploration journey. With NASA's Artemis mission planning to land in 2024, European Space Agency's PROSPECT program, and China's International Lunar Research Station by 2035, establishing lunar water mining infrastructure is critical for all future missions.

Water on the Moon: Discovery Timeline

1961

Theoretical Possibility

Kenneth Watson published a paper describing how water ice could exist in permanently shadowed lunar craters

1994-1998

First Evidence

Clementine mission suggested ice in shadowed regions. Lunar Prospector found hydrogen concentrations at the poles

2008

Apollo Samples Revisited

Brown University researchers found hydrogen inside volcanic glass beads from Apollo samples

2018

Confirmed: Shadowed Regions

India's Chandrayaan-1 Moon Mineralogy Mapper provided first high-resolution confirmation of ice in PSCs

2020

Sunlit Surface Water

NASA's SOFIA discovered water on sunlit surface - 12 oz per cubic meter in Clavius crater

2023

Detailed Mapping

New SOFIA map shows water distribution across Moon's South Pole - the target for Artemis missions
